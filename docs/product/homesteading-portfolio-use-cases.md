## Purpose

The student may pursue a homesteading, farming, environmental science, or practical-skills direction. The system must help translate that work into credible academic evidence without flattening it into generic assignments.

## Example Evidence

- Soil testing notes.
- Garden or livestock project logs.
- Farm business accounting exercises.
- Equipment maintenance records.
- Photos of project stages.
- Fieldwork logs.
- Reading logs connected to agriculture, ecology, biology, business, or civics.
- Written reflections, research summaries, and presentations.
- External course certificates or community-learning records.

## Course Translation

Practical work can support courses such as:

- Biology and Soil Science.
- Environmental Science.
- Farm Business Accounting.
- Practical Arts.
- Entrepreneurship.
- Civics, Economics, or Community Studies.
- Technical Writing or Research Writing.

## Requirement Mapping

The same course or project may map to multiple requirement areas with different coverage levels. For example, a soil-science project may map to science as primary, writing as secondary, and mathematics as supporting.

## Portfolio Expectations

Portfolio evidence should preserve:

- Date or date range.
- Course or learning context.
- Evidence type.
- Parent/student notes.
- Requirement tags where relevant.
- Attached files or photos.
- Whether the artifact is selected for official export.
